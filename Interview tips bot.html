8 Quick Tips to Remember

Start with these essential tips for interview success:

Before Your Interview
1 Know Yourself & What You Will Bring ▼

Understand your unique value proposition. Know your strengths, experiences, and how they align with the role. Be ready to articulate what you bring to the position and the company.

2 Come Prepared for Key Questions ▼

Prepare answers for common questions:

  • "Tell me about yourself"
  • "What are your strengths and weaknesses?"
  • "Why did you apply for this role?"
  • "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
  • "Tell me about a problem you solved at work"

Practice your answers out loud to build confidence.

3 Practice with a Friend or Family Member ▼

Conduct mock interviews with someone you trust. Get feedback on your answers, body language, and confidence level. Repeat this process multiple times to feel more prepared.

4 Research the Organisation ▼

Learn about the company's mission, values, recent news, and industry position. Understanding the organisation shows initiative and enthusiasm. Read reviews on sites like Glassdoor to learn about company culture and what others are saying about the employer.

5 Review the Position Description ▼

Print out or save the job description and underline specific skills the employer is looking for. Think about examples from your experience that align with these requirements. Be ready to discuss how your background makes you a great fit.

On the Interview Day
1 Remember: The Interview Starts as Soon as You Leave the House ▼

Project a professional image from the moment you leave home:

  • You never know who you might bump into
  • Be friendly and confident in your commute
  • Avoid negative conversations or complaints
  • Make conversation with the receptionist and other staff
  • Don't cram facts at the last minute—arrive early and relax
2 Dress Appropriately ▼

Choose professional attire that matches the company culture. When in doubt, dress more formally than you would for the job. Ensure your clothes are clean, well-fitted, and appropriate for the industry.

3 Be On Time and Organised ▼

In-person: Arrive 10 minutes early. Allow time for parking, finding the building, and a comfort break.

Online: Log in 5 minutes early to test your technology, lighting, and background.

Have all necessary documents, notes, and your phone charged.

4 Treat Everyone You Meet as Your Interviewer ▼

Be polite and friendly to everyone—receptionists, security staff, other employees. Your behaviour with all staff is observed and reported. Everyone contributes to the interview process assessment.

During the Interview
1 Maintain Positive Body Language ▼

Key points:

  • Sit up straight and maintain good posture
  • Make eye contact with your interviewer(s)
  • Smile and nod to show engagement
  • Avoid crossing your arms or fidgeting
  • Use natural hand gestures to emphasize points
2 Practice Active Listening ▼

Listen carefully to the interviewer's questions before responding. Take a moment to think about your answer. Ask for clarification if needed. Show genuine interest in what they're saying.

3 Be Honest ▼

Don't exaggerate or make up experience. If you don't know something, say so and explain how you'd learn it. Honesty builds trust and shows integrity.

4 Prepare Questions to Ask ▼

Ask thoughtful questions such as:

  • "What does a typical day in this role look like?"
  • "How would you describe the team culture?"
  • "What are the key priorities for this position?"
  • "How do you measure success in this role?"
  • "What opportunities are there for professional development?"
5 Present Professionally ▼

Speak clearly and at a moderate pace. Avoid filler words like "um" and "like". Use proper grammar. Show enthusiasm for the role and company. Let your personality shine while maintaining professionalism.

After the Interview
1 Thank Them & Ask About Next Steps ▼

At the end of the interview, thank them for their time. Ask what the next steps in the process are and when you can expect to hear back. This shows genuine interest and professionalism.

2 Follow Up After the Job Interview ▼

Send a follow-up email within 24 hours:

  • Thank the interviewer(s) for their time
  • Reiterate your interest in the position
  • Briefly mention a specific topic you discussed
  • Highlight why you're a good fit for the role
  • Keep it professional and concise (2-3 paragraphs)
3 Frequently Asked Questions ▼

Q: How long should I wait before following up?

A: Send your thank-you email within 24 hours. If you haven't heard back after the timeline they provided, wait 1-2 days then send a polite inquiry.

Q: Should I call or email?

A: Email is preferred unless they specifically said they'd call. It gives them a written record and allows them to respond at their convenience.

Q: What if I made a mistake during the interview?

A: Don't dwell on it. If it was significant, you can briefly acknowledge it in your follow-up email and provide the correct information. Most mistakes are minor and won't derail your chances.
